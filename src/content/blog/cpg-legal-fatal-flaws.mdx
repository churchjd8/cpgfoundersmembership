I've built 8 companies and invested in dozens more. The pattern I see over and over is founders getting tripped up by legal issues that were completely avoidable. These aren't edge cases. These are the four legal fatal flaws that I've watched destroy early-stage CPG brands.

## 1. Signing Long-Term Contracts

Here's a rule I live by: never sign a contract longer than one year when you're early-stage. Your business is going to change dramatically in the next 12 months. Your co-manufacturer might not be the right fit. Your distributor might not perform. Your broker might not deliver.

When you lock yourself into a 2-3 year deal, you're handcuffing a company that needs maximum flexibility. I've seen founders stuck paying a co-manufacturer they've outgrown because they signed a 3-year agreement to get a slightly better per-unit price. That "savings" cost them hundreds of thousands in opportunity cost.

**The fix:** Keep every contract to 12 months or less. If a vendor won't work with you on those terms, that tells you something about how much they value the relationship.

## 2. Ignoring Evergreen Contracts

This one is sneaky. An evergreen contract auto-renews unless you give formal written notice - usually 60-90 days before the renewal date. Miss that window by even one day, and you're locked in for another full term.

I've seen founders accidentally renew broker agreements, warehouse leases, and co-manufacturing deals simply because nobody put the cancellation deadline on the calendar. By the time they realized it, they were stuck for another year with a partner that wasn't working.

**The fix:** The day you sign any contract, put the cancellation notice deadline on your calendar with multiple reminders. Treat it like a fundraising deadline - because it is one.

## 3. Giving Personal Guarantees

Don't do it. I've skirted personal guarantees for 40 years, and you can too. Vendors, landlords, and lenders will push hard for a personal guarantee, especially when your company is small. They'll tell you it's standard. It's not - it's just what they prefer.

A personal guarantee means if your company fails, they come after your house, your savings, everything. CPG is inherently risky. You're already putting your career and time on the line. Don't put your family's financial security on the line too.

**The fix:** Push back. Hard. Offer a larger security deposit, prepayment, or shorter initial terms instead. If they absolutely won't budge, find another vendor. There's always another vendor.

## 4. Trademark Conflicts

This one can be truly devastating. You spend two years building brand awareness, getting into retail, building a consumer following - and then you get a cease-and-desist letter because another food brand (even in an unrelated category) has a similar name.

Now you're looking at a complete rebrand. New packaging, new marketing materials, new retailer presentations, new consumer education. I've seen this cost brands $500K+ and set them back 18 months. Some never recover.

**The fix:** Before you spend a single dollar on branding, do a comprehensive trademark search. Not just a quick Google search - hire a trademark attorney to do a full search across all food and beverage categories. File your trademark application immediately. The few thousand dollars this costs is the best insurance policy you'll ever buy.
