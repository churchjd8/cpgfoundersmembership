## Key Stories & Anecdotes

### The Juni Amazon Story (Ryan presenting)
Ryan shares live data from a brand he and Jeff work on together (likely Juni, a beverage brand doing ~$500K/month on Amazon for 3+ years). Key data points:
- 73% of revenue comes from repeat customers -- $4.3M of nearly $6M total
- Customers who purchase 4+ times have an average lifetime value of $293
- Initial customer acquisition cost is ~$30 (break-even on first purchase)
- The March 2025 cohort hit a 42% repeat rate within 12 months
- Median days between first and second purchase: 23 days
- 80-85% of sales are mix packs vs. single flavors
- Subscribe and Save numbers on mix packs are "tremendous" compared to single flavors
- Even at month 18, 3.3% of the original cohort is still purchasing
- January cohorts show a dip in repeat rates -- "New Year, New Me" buyers who try a product on a health kick and don't come back
- July cohorts also dip because of Prime Day trial buyers who grab a deal and never return
- From the March cohort: 634 customers bought 2-3 times, 534 bought 4+ times; the 4+ buyers are worth $310 each

### The Seaweed Soup Case Study (Austin presenting)
Austin walks through a Korean seaweed soup brand (broth rituals) that G7 took from ~$5K/month to ~$400K/month in revenue in less than 8 months:
- Started with one SKU, one basic landing page
- Site converts at ~6% (vs. industry norm of 2-3%)
- Discovered through customer feedback that 20% of bounced visitors were vegan -- created a vegan option that materially lifted conversion
- Built audience-specific landing pages: general, moms (postpartum/pregnancy), Korean/Asian-inspired, and wellness/gut health
- Each landing page has matched pop-ups, email flows, and SMS flows segmented by audience
- Ran a test on a drinks brand where they sold out 120,000 units in ~3 days, with 75% being one single mixed pack SKU

## Tactical Advice & Frameworks

### Repeat Rate as the North Star Metric
- You can measure repeat rate on DTC (Amazon or Shopify) almost immediately -- unlike brick and mortar where you need $20K+/month in Nielsen data
- Within the first 3 months of launching, you should see at least 15% repeat rate to know "you've got something there"
- If you look at data for 3 months and nobody's coming back, no amount of button-pushing on the back end will fix it -- it's a product problem (price, flavor, format)
- Target: 3x ratio between CAC and 12-month customer lifetime value (e.g., $25 CAC to $100 LTV)
- A 2% improvement in repeat rate (e.g., 33% to 35%) can have massive revenue impact
- "Do you want growth or do you want profit? Do you want the growth now and the profit later?"

### Mix Packs Are King
- On Amazon, mix packs should be 70%+ of your inventory allocation
- On Shopify/DTC, same pattern -- 75% of sales from one mixed pack SKU in a test
- Mix pack dominance doesn't change over time -- "three years on Juni doing $500K a month and it hasn't changed"
- Subscribe and Save numbers on mix packs far outpace single flavors
- Founders consistently under-allocate to mix packs despite being told, then scramble when they run short
- This pattern holds at retail too, especially for new/emerging brands

### Amazon Ad Spend Strategy
- Big brands can afford $7-8/click on high-volume generic keywords; small brands cannot
- Target: $3/click or less with 10% conversion rate to keep CAC manageable
- You will lose money on the first purchase -- that's normal and expected (example: -$7.22 on first order)
- By 2nd-3rd purchase, you're up $3.27; by 4th+ purchase, up $58
- TACOS (Total Advertising Cost of Sales) should improve over time as repeat customers stack
- Use the Search Query Performance Report to separate branded vs. non-branded search terms; track branded term volume as a proxy for brand awareness/halo effect from off-site marketing
- Auto campaigns are a good starting point to collect data on which keywords convert

### Meta/Shopify Conversion Framework
- If your site converts at 1%, you're "hurting pretty bad"
- Brand new brands should target 3%+ conversion rate
- Beautiful websites that don't convert are a common and expensive mistake -- "I've seen people spend $30-40K on a website that looks beautiful and converts at 1%"
- "Your website, your email, your SMS is like a bucket full of holes. You're just pouring water through it, and you're just trying to plug the holes as fast as possible."

### Audience-Specific Landing Pages (Austin's Process)
1. Start with a basic, proven-converting landing page
2. Run a new audience segment (e.g., moms) to that basic page first -- don't build a custom page yet
3. If the basic page converts at 3-4% for that audience, you've validated market fit
4. Only then copy the page and customize copy/imagery for that specific audience
5. Match the pop-up, email flows, and SMS flows to the audience segment
6. Each audience goes into its own email segment with tailored education content

### Email & SMS Flow Architecture
- First touchpoint: pop-up offering 10% off in exchange for email (two-step: email first, then phone number)
- 5-10% of sessions sign up for email; 2-5% for SMS
- SMS is more effective per-subscriber than email because people check texts more than email
- 7 automated welcome emails over ~1-2 weeks educating on the product, founder story, recipes, benefits
- Browse abandonment trigger: 2-3 emails if they viewed a product but left
- Add-to-cart abandonment trigger: 2-3 more emails/SMS
- Checkout abandonment trigger: additional follow-up
- Remarketing via email/SMS costs $1-2 per customer vs. $5-10 for ad remarketing
- "94-95% of customers don't purchase on the first visit" -- you must nurture them
- Don't be afraid of over-emailing; just make sure flow logic prevents customers from getting hit by welcome + abandoned cart + browse abandonment simultaneously

### Subscription & AOV Optimization (Buy Box Strategy)
- Never use simple quantity selectors (+/-) -- use visual tiered pricing instead
- Default selection should load on 2-box (not 1-box) on page load
- One-time purchases get zero discount -- no incentive to buy one-time
- Subscription gets 10-15% off for 1-box, scaling up to 20-25% for 4-box
- Recommended discount tiers for most brands: one-time 1-box = 0%, one-time 3-box = ~10%, subscribe 1-box = ~10%, subscribe 3-box = ~15%, subscribe 4-box = ~20%+
- Gift with purchase for subscribers to differentiate Shopify from Amazon experience
- Always offer free shipping -- adding shipping costs at checkout kills conversion

### Amazon vs. Shopify: How They Work Together
- They don't cannibalize each other "if you do it right" -- they should help each other
- Shopify gives you more control over user experience, discounts, promotions, gift with purchase
- Amazon has higher organic traffic and better default loyalty/repeat rates because of platform trust
- Meta ad campaigns to Shopify create a "halo effect" on Amazon -- you can't directly attribute it, but branded search volume on Amazon rises when off-site campaigns run
- You can use Amazon as a 3PL for Shopify orders (via FBA)
- Buy with Prime button on Shopify exists but doesn't drive meaningful volume

### Where to Spend First
- Always start with Shopify (not other platforms) -- it's far ahead in buying/purchasing/AI
- Recommended starting ad budget: $5K-10K/month ($100-200/day) on Meta
- Similar budget on Amazon
- $10/day is too little -- "you can learn with $10 a day, but it's going to take you years"
- Google is secondary -- "do it very light because Google is actually going to get sales from Meta and take credit for them"
- Allocate maybe 10-15% of total spend to Google to "protect" branded search terms
- TikTok is "becoming a massive platform" but "you got to know what you're doing"

### Hiring an Amazon Agency
- "Don't have the A-team selling you and the B-team or C-team showing up to do the implementation"
- Demand cohort analysis -- many agencies don't show it, and it's the most valuable data
- Performance marketing is fundamentally different from traditional CPG marketing -- it's quantitative, measurable, transaction-level

### When to Optimize Your Site vs. When to Launch Ads
- Do conversion optimization BEFORE spending on ads -- "do as much as you can out of the gate"
- "Make sure your website, your email, and your SMS flows are top tier before you spend a dime"
- Don't wait for perfect branding to start -- use baseline conversion best practices from day one
- Full-funnel control (website + ads + content + email/SMS) under one team prevents siloed problems
